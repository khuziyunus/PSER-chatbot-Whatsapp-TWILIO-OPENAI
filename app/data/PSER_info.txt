 Punjab Socio-Economic Registry (PSER), 
 a government initiative in Punjab, Pakistan, 
 to create a database of households for better targeting of social welfare programs. 
 It is a department-level project under the Planning and Development Board and is overseen by the 
 Senior Minister for Planning and Development, Mariyyum Aurangzeb. 
 The PSER initiative uses a comprehensive survey to collect data on 
 demographics, assets, and living conditions to identify families in need.
---------
Purpose and functions
Centralized Data Repository: The PSER acts as a centralized, real-time database for 
detailed household information.
Proxy Means Test (PMT): It generates a PMT score to help identify and prioritize
families eligible for government assistance programs.
Improved Welfare Programs: The data helps the government design more responsive, transparent, and accurately targeted welfare programs.
Facilitating Access: It helps provide access to subsidized health services, education, and financial assistance, such as the CM Punjab Ration Card program.
